## 0:00 - 2:00 | Introduction: The Core Problem

**[ACTION]** Start at the absolute top of the page. Do not scroll yet. Let the ambient background glowing orbs and the hero image set the mood. Keep your mouse still initially.

**Presenter:**
"Good [morning/afternoon/evening], everyone. Today, I want to ask you a fundamental question about how we measure intelligence and capability on a global scale."

**[ACTION]** Slowly move your mouse across the title "Lost in Translation" to demonstrate the gradient text effect.

**Presenter:**
"Standardized testing, like the PISA exam, is designed to measure student capability. But what happens when the test itself speaks a foreign language? 

Welcome to our project, *Lost in Translation*. Today we will explore the impact of the language of instruction on PISA outcomes, specifically comparing the Philippines with top-performing nations. Imagine trying to solve a complex math problem while simultaneously translating the question in your head. This 'cognitive gap' is exactly what non-native English speakers face. Let's explore how language policies shape educational destinies."

**[ACTION]** Hover your mouse over the 'Explore the Analysis' button to trigger the glow, scale, and arrow animation. Then slowly click it, or manually scroll down to the framework section.

## 2:00 - 4:00 | The Analytical Framework

**[ACTION]** Ensure the "Analytical Framework" glass card is centered on screen. Slowly move your mouse in a circle *inside* the card to show off the mouse-tracking spotlight glow effect.

**Presenter:**
"To ensure our comparative analysis is both structured and comprehensive, we utilize the Bray & Thomas framework from 1995 (Bray & Thomas, 1995). 

We aren't just looking at test scores in a vacuum. By integrating cross-national comparison and comparative policy analysis, we are examining how entire national education systems operate. We will break this down into four key areas: the Context they exist in, the Inputs they receive, the Processes they use, and finally, the Outcomes they produce."

**Presenter:**
"**Cultural:** Societal values and colonial histories deeply influence language preferences. In the Philippines, English is culturally viewed as the language of success and global competitiveness. ## 9:00 - 11:30 | Processes: Delivery and Assessment

**[ACTION]** Scroll down to the dark "Processes" grid displaying the three icons. 

**Presenter:**

**[ACTION]** Hover your mouse directly over the **Pedagogy** card to activate its bottom-right colored glow and spotlight effect.

**Presenter:**
"**Pedagogy:** Teaching methods change significantly when there's a language barrier. Educators often have to shift away from interactive, student-centered learning, resorting instead to lecture-based rote memorization simply to compensate for their students' vocabulary deficits."

**[ACTION]** Hover over the **Curriculum** card.

**Presenter:**
"**Curriculum:** The curriculum delivery must align with the linguistic realities of the students, but often it assumes a level of English proficiency that isn't present."

**[ACTION]** Hover over the **Assessment** card. Tap the desk or use slight hand gestures offline to emphasize this crucial point.

**Presenter:**
"**Assessment:** This is the crux of the issue. When a Filipino student takes the PISA exam, the test often measures their English reading comprehension simultaneously with their scientific or mathematical aptitude. This creates a massive, unfair **cognitive load** that students in top-performing, mother-tongue-tested nations simply do not face."

## 11:30 - 13:00 | The Cognitive Load: A Practical Example

**[ACTION]** Scroll down to the "VS" Comparison section. Hover your mouse over the blue "The Philippines" card on the left.

**Presenter:**
"Let's look at a practical example. Here is a sample 8th-grade science question. A Filipino student must first decode unfamiliar English vocabularyâ€”like 'fluid' or 'molecular motion'. Then, they mentally translate that sentence structure into their native tongue, perhaps Tagalog or Cebuano. *Only then* can they access their scientific knowledge to actually solve the problem. As you can see, the cognitive load is immense."

**[ACTION]** Move your hover to the pink "Finland" card on the right. Point out the short cognitive load bar at the bottom.

**Presenter:**
"Now contrast this with a student in Finland, taking the exact same test, but in Finnish. They bypass the decoding and translation phases entirely. They immediately access their scientific knowledge to solve the problem. The cognitive load is minimal, allowing their true aptitude to shine through."
